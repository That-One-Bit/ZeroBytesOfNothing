Mesopotamian Life

What was life like as a farmer?

Life as a farmer was hard, but shared. After the city had figured out how to deal with the flooding of the Tigris and Euphrates rivers, your job became more complex. You (and the other farmers) had to maintain the complex irrigation systems across towns, but it was for the people. From day to night, you worked on the farms, barely getting any breaks, but it was a good life. A wealthy life. Your farm had made you thousands of sheckles over the years. Most farms would thrive as long as the other

What was life like as trader?

As a trader, you wandered across Mesopotamia, observing how other city-states worked in contrast to yours. It was a fine life, making you a surplus of money. Even though you worked from day to night, you thought it was fine. You never had friends to help share the load, but it was fine. You couldn't have much, since you were always moving, and the days passed by. It was possible that being a farmer or blacksmith was easier, but this was the life you chose. On the other end, the happy one, you always had enough. It was enough, right?

What was life like as a blacksmith?

Life as a blacksmith. It was a hard job but it was worth it. For years, your father had taught you how to forge weapons and make sure that they were ready for the king’s guard. You had aspired to do this job and you finally made it! It’s much more dangerous than it seems, but you think you can handle it. Just because you don’t get many days off doesn’t mean you can’t have fun. Since you lived in a village, you always had enough food. You had room for furniture and other items, so you had them. It was a hard life, but you loved it. But what would happen if a war broke out?

Life as one of the King’s Soldiers

Being one of the king’s soldiers, you had little time for yourself. There was constant training, constant money spending, and constant loyalty. Had you forgotten to show up, you would be executed immediately. There were constant battles being fought, all in the name of fun, but these battles were harsh. Warriors fell from the wars and people willing to fight were dropping out. Your life was fine, you got food, supplies, and enough. If a war was to happen, you wouldn’t be so lucky…

If a war was to happen

If a war was to break out between city-states, and you were a soldier, then you would be sent to either fight for more land/water or defend your land. You would’ve known that it was dangerous. You would’ve known that you may not make it back. But it was for your people, and that led you to win the battle. After the war, prices for many things would’ve gone up. It was common for this to happen, as many resources were used during the war for the soldiers. Peace treaties were formed, and many alliances were made to keep others safe. Maybe it wouldn’t happen again?

The Irrigation Systems

If the other city-states were to block the irrigation canals, then your city wouldn’t have water for crops. This could very much lead to wars, as they are taking advantage of the collaborations needed for farming in such a difficult environment. Your city-state would most likely lose, as you have less resources to work with. Less food means prices go up. Less food means less energy for the soldiers. That leads to the end and death of a city-state.
